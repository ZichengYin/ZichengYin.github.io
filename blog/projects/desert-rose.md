## Project Introduction

"Desert Rose" is a music timing training game that transforms abstract musical duration concepts into an immersive gameplay experience. Inspired by games like "Fruit Ninja" and "Suika Game", players combine eighth notes to form quarter notes, quarter notes to half notes, and so on. Each successful combination triggers corresponding sound effects, providing dual visual and auditory feedback to help beginners intuitively understand note duration relationships.

## Tech Stack

- **Development Tools**: Unity, Cubase
- **Art Design**: Original Q-style note assets, war-themed UI redesign
- **Sound Design**: Analog vocal synthesis (v1.0), gunfire and explosion sounds recorded from games and real objects (v2.0)
- **Background Music**: "Nuvole Bianche" (contemporary piano piece)

## My Role

- Art design and UI production
- Sound effect recording and editing
- Theme concept and narrative design

## Project Highlights

- **Version 1.0**: Candy-colored visuals with vocal synthesis sounds, validating the feasibility of gamified timing training through "sound-image-operation" triple feedback.
- **Version 2.0**: Themed around war and peace, transforming visuals from ruins to dawn skies with rose elements. Sound effects changed from vocals to gunfire — short thirty-second notes as bursts, long double-whole notes as cannon fire.
- **Original Art Design**: All game art is original, incorporating rose motifs to symbolize resilient life amidst gunfire.
- **Experimental Sound Design**: Gunfire sounds recorded from "Hunt Showdown", as well as self-recorded balloon pops and book slaps, edited in Cubase for precise duration control.
- **Emotional Narrative**: Uses the contemporary piano piece "Nuvole Bianche" — a song that once moved soldiers to lay down their weapons during the Ukraine conflict — as the background music, conveying a message of peace.

## Project Achievement & Future Plan

- **Current Achievement**: Completed two thematic versions (candy vs. war). The v2.0 war theme has been fully redesigned in visuals and sound effects.
- **Future Plan**:
  1. Introduce dynamic difficulty — adjust note falling speed based on player error rate
  2. Expand the question/note combination types
  3. Further explore the educational potential of using sound brutality to reinforce duration perception
